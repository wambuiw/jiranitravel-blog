Stargazing in Kenya: Africa’s Next Big Travel Experience

Posted on June 25, 2025 | By Jirani Team

Forget hotel lights and car horns. In Kenya’s northern deserts and remote savannahs, the sky becomes a giant dome of stars, planets, and meteor showers. Astro-tourism — the growing trend of traveling to dark sky locations — is putting places like Turkana and Laikipia on the global stargazing map.

During dry season (July–October), visibility is crystal clear. Lay back and catch the Milky Way with your naked eye, or join a guided astronomy tour hosted by local experts using telescopes.

Even local communities are now offering night sky storytelling experiences, blending astronomy with myth and heritage. A travel experience that’s both cosmic and cultural.

Best Stargazing Spots

  • Ngurunit in Northern Kenya
  • Lake Turkana Shores
  • Ol Pejeta Conservancy
  • Samburu Sopa Lodge
